## üéØ Communication Objectives

1. **Clarity**: All reviewers understand their role and expectations

## üë• Stakeholders & Communication Preferences

### Primary Stakeholders

**Project Lead**
- Role: Review owner, decision maker, final approver
- Communication: Daily sync (Slack/Email), weekly status updates
- Escalation: Should be informed of any issues immediately

**Reviewers (6 total)**
- Role: Independent evaluators, subject matter experts
- Communication: Email for formal materials, Slack/Teams for quick questions
- Escalation: Contact project lead for blockers or concerns

**Broader Team** (Documentation team, engineering leads)
- Role: Stakeholders who care about Phase 5 execution
- Communication: Final approval announcement via email + team meeting
- Information: High-level outcomes, not detailed review comments

## üõë Escalation & Issue Management

### If Reviewer Doesn't Submit Review

**Timeline**:
- Dec 30, 5 PM: Review deadline
- Dec 30, 5:15 PM: Send reminder email
- Dec 30, 6 PM: Call or message reviewer directly
- Dec 31, 8 AM: If still no review, prepare to proceed with available feedback

**Actions**:
1. Contact reviewer directly
2. Offer to discuss concerns verbally
3. Note in meeting that review is missing
4. Proceed with synthesis of available feedback

---

### If Reviewer Can't Attend Alignment Meeting

**Timeline**:
- Dec 29: Confirm attendance
- Dec 30: Send reminder with meeting details
- Dec 31, 12 PM: Final confirmation requested

**Backup Plan**:
- Reviewer can submit written feedback in advance
- Lead synthesizes key points in meeting
- Reviewer can review meeting notes and approve async

---

### If Major Issues Discovered During Review

**Escalation Path**:
1. Reviewer flags issue in review or meeting
2. Lead acknowledges urgency
3. Quick discussion with relevant stakeholders
4. Determine if issue requires:
   - Minor plan adjustment (approved by lead)
   - Major revision (requires team re-review)
   - Scope reduction (discussed in meeting)

**Communication**: Transparent discussion in alignment meeting
